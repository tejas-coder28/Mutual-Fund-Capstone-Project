## Project Insights & Observations

### 1. Risk Analysis (VaR & CVaR)
Aggressive equity funds hit the highest Value at Risk numbers. This means there is a clear 5% chance of seeing sharp daily drops when things go south. Looking at the CVaR metrics, when a market crash actually breaches that 5% line, the losses get significantly worse because the portfolios are heavily focused on just a few major sectors.

### 2. Sharpe Ratio Performance Over Time
The 90-day rolling Sharpe ratio chart clearly shows that fund efficiency fluctuates constantly. A fund might look incredible with a static Sharpe ratio above 1.5 during a bull run, but it drops rapidly during market corrections. Relying on a single snapshot of data doesn't tell the full story of a fund's actual stability.

### 3. Investor Cohort Trends
The data shows that older cohorts (people who started investing back in 2021 or 2022) have much larger average SIP sizes and have kept most of their money in the market. On the other hand, the newer cohorts start smaller but show a huge preference for low-cost passive index funds over actively managed ones.

### 4. User Churn & SIP Gaps
When checking users who have stuck around for at least 6 transactions, about 14% of them have gaps between their investment dates that stretch past 35 days. Flagging these specific accounts as "at-risk" lets us spot potential churn early and send out reminders before they stop investing entirely.

### 5. Sector Concentration Metrics (HHI)
Calculating the Herfindahl Index proves that high portfolio concentration brings massive volatility. Funds with an HHI score over 2,500 rely way too heavily on specific banking and IT stocks. They make incredible gains when those sectors rally, but they take a massive hit when those specific industries slow down.
